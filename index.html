Work

Publicis Sapient

When I joined Publicis Sapient in October 2018, I started working on the RBS account. As a team of four developers, we built a POC, in a series of 6 2-week sprints, for a new ‘Credit Wallet’ app for NatWest. We built a progressive web app using React.js, Redux and Styled Components. We used storybook to create a library of components which the app could then consume and use where needed.

After finishing on RBS I moved to the Nissan account where my role has been to work within a team developing the “build your own car” part to the website, known as the configurator. This project involves converting years of legacy code, originally written in Backbone.js, to Vue.js, to improve performance. During the migration, we have also taken the chance to do some redesign and to enhance the APIs, freeing up the front-end of the heavy business logic. I have led the development of a series of pages and features and am the longest standing developer on the team. With this, I was able to communicate our achievements to the rest of the technology department, about 100 people, through a presentation at one of our monthly meetings. This project has involved delivering to tight deadlines with constantly changing client requirements.

The Spice Drawer

This is a website I have been working on in my spare time just for myself, to store all my go-to recipes for easy accessibility. It is a work in progress to which I like to build on when I have the time; adding recipes and building to the functionality. My idea was to start lean and form a very simple website where I can just view my favourite recipes where I have simplified their methods and made a few tweaks. To build the front-end I have used Vue.js and Node.js for the back-end which hooks up to a Firebase Realtime Database. This allows me to add recipes on the go from anywhere. Then finally I am using Netlify for the deployment.
